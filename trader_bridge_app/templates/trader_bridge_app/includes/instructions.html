The Stock
  • The stock pays a dividend at the end of each period. Dividends are equally likely to be {{ dividend_values_text }}, with the expected dividend being {{ expected_dividend }} per period.
  • Fundamental value declines over time and equals expected dividend times remaining periods (including current period). It starts at {{ fundamental_value_start }} in period 1 and declines by {{ fundamental_value_step }} each period, regardless of the realized dividend. After the final dividend is paid, the stock is worthless.
Trading Rules

Trading uses a limit order book. You may submit buy/sell limit orders or trade immediately against resting orders.

  • You may buy shares only if you have sufficient cash.
  • You may sell shares only if you own them (no short selling).
  • You cannot borrow additional cash.
Price Forecasting

At the end of each period, after dividends are paid, you submit a point forecast for next-period end price and report confidence. If your forecast is within {{ forecast_bonus_threshold_pct }}% of the realized price, you earn a bonus of E${{ forecast_bonus_amount }}.

Payment
  • Final payoff equals your cash holdings at the end of a randomly selected market, including period {{ payoff_period }} dividends, plus forecast bonuses earned across all markets.
  • Experimental currency converts to real money at rate: 1 point = {{ exchange_rate_text }} (session currency units).
  • Quiz bonus per correct answer: {{ quiz_bonus_per_correct_text }} points.
Important Notes
  • Your trading screen shows order book, recent transactions, cash balance, and share holdings.
  • Each period lasts exactly {{ trading_day_duration }} minute{% if trading_day_duration != 1 %}s{% endif %}. A timer shows time remaining.
  • Markets are independent. Each new market starts with a fresh endowment and the stock fundamental value resets.
Training

Before main trading, you will watch a short interface explainer and complete a comprehension quiz. You must answer all comprehension questions correctly before proceeding.
